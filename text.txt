The road curled through the forest like a forgotten thought, its edges swallowed by creeping moss and shadows that moved when you weren’t looking directly at them. Elias kept walking, though he couldn’t remember when he had first stepped onto the path or what waited at its end. The air carried a faint hum, as if the trees themselves were whispering in a language just beyond comprehension. Every so often, he would pass a weathered signpost pointing in conflicting directions, each arrow carved with symbols that seemed to rearrange when he blinked. Still, something deep and insistent pulled him forward—not hope, not fear, but a quiet certainty that turning back was no longer an option.
